Conversations: A Guide to Packing Your DNA

Genetic material is not randomly organized within the nucleus of a cell. How this organization occurs and why it matters are questions that Cell editor Marta Koch posed to Mitchell Guttman, Job Dekker, and Stavros Lomvardas. The 4D Nucleome grantees Drs. Job Dekker, Mitchell Guttman, and Stavros Lomvardas discuss the organization of genetic material in the nucleus, how it occurs, and why it matters.

A quick guide to 4DN visualization software

Plot a course through the genome: inspired by Google Maps, a suite of tools is allowing researchers to chart the complex conformations of chromosomes.

Integrated Approaches for Spatial and Temporal Mapping of Gene Activity in Cells and Tissues

When: September 20, 2017, Bethesda
The purpose of this workshop is to introduce expertise and technology to the consortium that would be important to help achieve the 4DN goals. The workshop will focus on spatial and temporal aspects of chromatin structure that lead to activation. The goal would be to: 1) identify areas of collaboration for the 4DN network, 2) identify potential areas of collaboration with other programs, 3) provide an opportunity to have a dialog between 4DN investigators and other researchers working in this field.

ISMB Tutorial: 3D Genome Data Processing, Analysis, and Visualization

When: July 21, 2017, Prague
Offered by 4DN members: Leonid Mirny, Nils Gehlenborg, and Jian Ma
Goals and Objectives: 1) To introduce the theoretical concepts related to 3D genome data analysis, 2) To familiarize participants with the data types, analysis pipeline, and common tools for analysis and visualization of 3D genome data, 3) To provide a hands on experience in data analysis by walking through some common use cases of existing tools for data analysis and visualization.
